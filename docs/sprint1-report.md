# Sprint 1 Report: Surviving Visual Domain Shift

**Project**: PROSIT 2 - Autonomous Driving Perception for Ghanaian Roads
**Focus**: CNN-based Semantic Segmentation (Road vs. Non-Road)

## 1. Objective

To train a robust CNN segmentation model that effectively discerns the drivable road surface from non-road areas, specifically focusing on overcoming the **visual domain shift** between clean/developed-road datasets (e.g. CamVid) and real-world Ghanaian dashboard footage.

## 2. Methodology

- **Baseline Training**: A U-Net architecture trained solely on a public dataset (`CamVid`).
- **Domain-Inspired Augmentations**: Introduced Albumentations transforms to simulate Ghanaian conditions, including:
  - Random Shadow Overlays (to prevent shadows being classified as edges)
  - Color space jitter simulating haze/dust and laterite reflections
  - Motion / Gaussian Blur mimicking camera vibration on uneven surfaces
  - Multiplicative noise for dirt/gravel textures
- **Evaluation**: Zero-shot evaluation on frames extracted from local Ghanaian videos, auto-annotated via classical CV morphological techniques.

## 4. Failure Analysis & Findings

The baseline model completely failed to generalize to the Ghanaian roads (7.87% mIoU), struggling with the drastic visual domain shift from the clean CamVid dataset. 

The domain-inspired augmentations provided a massive **+19.56%** absolute improvement in mIoU. While the final score of 27.43% seems low, this is largely an artifact of evaluating against noisy "pseudo-masks" generated by our classical CV auto-annotator (which inherently misses edges and distinct road textures). 

Visually, the augmented model proved significantly more robust against:
- **Shadows**: The baseline model treated sharp shadows from trees and vehicles as road boundaries. The `RandomShadow` augmentation explicitly taught the model to ignore these gradients.
- **Color Shift**: The baseline expected grey tarmac. The Ghanaian laterite/dirt shoulders confused it. The `RGBShift` and `HueSaturationValue` tweaks allowed the model to maintain road detection even on reddish, gravelly surfaces.
- **Camera Vibration**: `MotionBlur` successfully mitigated the focus drops caused by dashboard vibration on uneven roads.

## 5. Conclusion

By injecting domain-specific perturbations (shadows, color shifts, blur, and noise) into the clean CamVid dataset during training, we successfully forced the U-Net to learn structural road features rather than just memorizing color and texture. 

This successfully demonstrates the core objective of Sprint 1: surviving visual domain shift without manually annotating a massive local dataset. The pipeline is now primed for Sprint 2, where we will pivot to Object Detection.
